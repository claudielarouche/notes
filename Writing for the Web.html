3 things to keep in mind when writing for the web

1 - Make it easy to find
    Unique, useful content
    Keywords that users are familiar with
    Keywords in both body and title
    Identify and write for audience
2 - Make it easy to scan
    Organize content to meet users needs
    Think through questions users will ask
    Chronological organization
    General first, then exception, conditions and other special info
    Limit levels to 3-4
    Address separate audiences separately
    Chunky content (short sections with breaks)
3 - Make it easy to understand
    Familiar words instead of jargon
    Short, simple sentences
    Active voice, write in the second person
    Avoid idioms like "belt-tightening"
    Avoid acronyms or abbreviations, unless all your readers know and use them
    Skip fluff, wastes user's time
    Use contractions, keep do not when it's important to make a point
    Address one person, not a group. You're only speaking to one person at a time.
    Use tables
    Use illustrations and graphics
Traps
    Don't post content online without "translating" it for the web
    You're not writing the next Globe & Mail best seller
    Don't use all caps or underlined text
    Don't use cross reference
