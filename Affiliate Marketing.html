It takes time and substantial effort to start earning good money

Click-through Rate (CTR): Dependant on how targeted your traffic is and the type of linking you'll be using (banners, text links, video, product links, reviews...)

Conversion Rate (CR): Percentage of traffic that converts to desired action

Average Order Value (AOV): Average affiliate-referred ticket, calculated as a sum total of all affiliate generated orders referred over a given period of time, and then divided by the number of orders

Payout: Commission or flat amount per sale/lead.

Reversal Rate (RR): Percentage of affiliate-referred transactions that get reversed by the merchant

Earnings per click (EPC): The metric that tells you what other affiliates are already making on this program

It is NOT possible to build a 100% secure passive income stream through affiliate marketing.

Startup costs: from $150 a year (website domain + hosting)
Time investment: 1-2 hours a day to a full work day

How affiliates can make money

Cost per sale (CPS) / Pay per sale (PPS): Affiliates get a percentage of the sale (80% of affiliate programs work this way)

Cost per lead (CPL) / Pay per lead (PPL): Affiliate receive compensation for referred leads (ex. forms submitted, subscriptions, free trials, etc.)

Cost per click (CPC) : Ceasing model (mainly due to large volume of fraud)

Pay per call: Advertisers pay affiliate for "hot leads" who call in

Types of affiliates

1. Content Publisher:
    Rely on content-saturated websites. Self-authored or user-generated content. Monetize sites with affiliate links, banners, widgets, text links, product links, etc. (example: http://digital-photography-school.com/popular-digital-cameras-and-gear/)
2. Coupon Affiliate:
    Rely on human psychology of trying to find a product at a discounted price. (example: couponcabin.com)
3. Data Feed Affiliates:
    Work with merchants' product feed (example: thefind.com)
4. Display Ads:
    Promote advertisers using display advertising. Includes retargeting.
5. E-mail affiliates:
    Market through e-mail. Includes remarketing.
6. Loyalty and Incentives:
    Offer incentives for desired actions. Cash back, points, virtual currency
7. Mobile or Pay per call:
    Uses mobile marketing and pay-per-call marketing. Bridge the gap between online and offline. May intertwine with location-based marketing.
8. Search engine marketing:
    Employe SEM to drive conversions. SEO, PPC or both.
9. Social Media:
    Leverages existing social media networks. Creates social network.
10. Video affiliates
    Focus on video creatives. Yield 29% higher dwell rate and 45% higher conversion rate.
Clicks are tracked by the platform
Conversion is tracked by a string of code on the merchant's confirmation page or thank you page

Prior to building website

1. Select your niche
    - Follow your passion
    - Monetize hot trends
    - Become key player / influencer
2. Identify Target Demographic (gender, age, income, children, religion, ethnicity, geographic data...)

3. Decide on type of Website
    The key is to provide your friends/followers with high-quality, problem-solving, or entertaining content that they need/want
4. Choose Promotional Methods

5. Select a platform

Note: a dedicated disclosure policy page, visible from every page of the website, should suffice, but you must have one

To generate a disclosure policy: http://disclosurepolicy.org/

Factors for selecting affiliate program

1. Website (ease of use, professionalism)
2. Reputation
3. Competitiveness
4. Market saturation
5. Payouts
6. Commission recurrence
7. Terms of service
8. Cookie life
9. Conversion rate
10. Earnings per click (this is the average of how much affiliates of this merchant earn on every 100 clicks they refer)
11. Reversal rate (rarely disclosed upfront)
12. Tracking/Reporting platform
13. Management/Approachability
14. Creatives
15. Tools

Enemies of money
Broken links
Leaks: Phone number, Live chat, links to related stores/sites/other merchants/other affiliates
Common banner mistakes: Poor graphics, unreadable fonts, Phone number/URL typed out, excessive animation, missing call-to-action, poor contrast, no line around the edge
