Web Content Checklist

Content strategy
- Define your goals and objectives
- Define your audience
- Understand your key audiences and their needs
- Monitor trands and stay relevant
- Topics should relate to keyword research
- Define your content angles
- Be original
- Scan your competition for untapped opportunities
- Leverage your customers for new content
- Leverage your network for new content
    - Vendors
    - Peers
    - Professional networks
- List out your broad content themes
    - Educational
    - Statistical
    - Technical
    - Procedural
    - Informational
- Implement and editorial calendar (chart that maps out upcoming publications and deadline dates for submissions and channel like Facebook, website, twitter...)
- Build your presence



Writing

 Use plain language.
    Keep your writing style simple, short, concise, consistent, active, positive and relevant.
    Avoid jargon and idioms.
    Explain references to legislation.
 Replace abbreviations with words.
    Use “program”, “Act” and other terms for second references.
 Use a consistent tone.
    Keep the same level of language.
    Write directly to the user.
    Use “we” only when it is clear who is speaking.
 Avoid contractions.
    Use “would not” or “it is” rather than “wouldn’t” or “it’s.”
Style
 Do not underline text.
    Use underline only for links.
 Use italics properly.
    Use italics only for titles of publications, acts, and in other instances as outlined in The Canadian Style and Le guide du rédacteur.
 Use bold sparingly.
    Remember that the more you use bold, the less effective it becomes.
 Use bullets and lists correctly.
    Use numbered lists sparingly.
    Use parallel grammatical structures.
    Use lower case for the first word (if not a pronoun) and do not use punctuate items
 Avoid using all caps.
    Unless a known acronym.
Structure

 Organize your content to help users complete tasks.
    Use the inverted pyramid structure to guide users to the information they need.
 Use page titles, headings and subheadings correctly.
    Ensure that page titles are unique.
    Keep page titles, headings and subheadings simple and short.
    Use page titles, headings and subheadings to show the page structure.
 Use sentence case.
    Capitalize only the first word and any proper noun in a title, heading or subheading.
Tables
 Use tables only to present data.
    Fill rows and columns with relevant data.
    Do not use only colour or texture to explain something.
    Avoid blank cells where possible.
 Include a heading.
    Include a brief heading that explains what information is in the table.
 Use at least one row header.
    Indicate the row header for the table.
 Simplify tables, if possible.
    Convert a complex table into one or more simpler tables.
    Include text instead to convey the same information.
Images and multimedia
 Use functional images within text.
    Use only if they help explain concepts within the text.
    Use only if they support task completion.
 Verify the rights to publish.
    Ensure that the appropriate authorization and licences have been obtained.
 Provide “alt” (alternative) text descriptions for images that convey meaningful information.
    Provide a clear, concise and descriptive explanation of what the image conveys.
 Provide long descriptions for charts, figures, graphs or complex graphics.
    Provide detailed text that explains all information being communicated.
    Provide the data tables used to generate charts or graphics.
 Provide transcripts for all multimedia content (audio, videos, games).
    Provide a transcript that includes all dialogue, narration, important sounds or actions that occur.
Links

 Make links descriptive.
    Use different linked text to point to different pages.
    Avoid vague terms such as “click here” or “more.”
    Avoid prose or promotional messaging as part of linked text.
 Ensure that links work.
    Bring users to the correct page, in their chosen language.
 Use links strategically.
    Cross-linking related information helps users and increases search engine rankings.
    Do not include too many links; users may leave the page without reading important information.
 Write link descriptions and introductory paragraphs consistently.
    Provide a summary of information or tasks to be accomplished on the page or by following the link.
    Write action-driven text such as “Search the database to learn more about…”
    Do not include links in introductory paragraphs or link descriptions.
